§ 1 — Investment Thesis

The Compounding Case

Croydon-based specialty foams manufacturer founded 1921, IPO 1993. World leader in supercritical (gas-blown) polyolefin and high-performance foams using a patented autoclave process that produces uniformly cellular, low-density foams competitors can't replicate at scale. Three segments: Polyolefin Foams (AZOTE — building, marine, packaging), High-Performance Products (ZOTEK — aerospace, medical, T-FIT cleanroom; Nike footwear cushioning), and MuCell Extrusion (licensed microcellular technology). FY2025 record results: revenue £158.5m (+7%), adj op profit £22.8m (+26%), op margin 14.4%. Nike represented ~40% of revenue — both the largest opportunity and the largest risk. November 2025 completed first-ever acquisition (OKC, Spanish technical foams, €36m / 7× EBITDA). Vietnam footwear plant commissioning late 2026. Medium-term target: £300m revenue, £60m op profit (vs current £158m / £22.8m). Stock at 426p, well off 479p high, trades on ~9.5× EV/EBITDA, ~13× P/E forward.

§ 5 — Key Risks

What Can Go Wrong

Risk — Nike concentration (40% of revenue)
Nike represents ~40% of group revenue and the dominant share of the highest-margin HPP segment. Any change in shoe-platform specifications, sourcing strategy, or relationship cooling would be catastrophic for earnings. Mitigant: exclusive supply contract extended to Dec 2029; product is engineered into specific shoe platforms (Vaporfly, Pegasus, Air Max franchises) with multi-year design cycles; Nike has invested alongside Zotefoams in Vietnam capacity, signalling commitment.
Risk — Nike footwear inventory normalisation in 2026
Management explicitly flagged that footwear demand will moderate in 2026 as Nike normalises channel inventory after multi-year build. Could mean flat to down HPP revenue in 2026. Mitigant: OKC acquisition + Transport & Smart Tech growth + medical can offset; medium-term Olympics 2028 cycle restoration.
Risk — ReZorce write-down credibility damage
£15.2m exceptional + £4.9m non-recurring to wind down ReZorce in late 2024 was a significant innovation failure. Damages management credibility on the broader "expanding beyond the core" thesis. Mitigant: New CFO (Wright, ex-Marshalls and Essentra), new NED (Clarke, Oct 2025); board has been refreshed; OKC represents disciplined M&A discipline (7× multiple, complementary).
Risk — US tariff exposure on Vietnam exports
Vietnam plant is built specifically to serve Nike US footwear assembly. US tariff regime on Vietnamese imports could pressure economics. Mitigant: CEO's public response — even with tariffs, Vietnamese labour is ~10% of US cost, so footwear assembly will not return onshore. Tariffs hit Nike, not Zotefoams.
Risk — Smaller-cap liquidity / index ineligibility
Average daily volume modest; not in any major index (FTSE SmallCap only). Institutional sponsorship limited. Mitigant: not actually a mitigant for institutional buyers — this is structurally what it is. Personal/retail-friendly; activist-unfriendly.

§ 7 — Moat & Compounding Mechanics

Structural Moat & Compounding Math

Applying Pat Dorsey's framework (The Little Book That Builds Wealth): which of the four sources of economic moat does this business have, is the moat sustainable, and does the compounding math actually work?
Moat Classification
Intangible (Process IP) + Switching Cost (Designed-In Specifications)
Patented supercritical autoclave process + multi-year integration into customer product platforms
Dorsey Test
PASS (single-customer caveat)
Supercritical autoclave manufacturing (intangible / process IP)
Zotefoams uses a two-stage nitrogen gas-blown autoclave process (rather than chemical blowing agents) that produces foams with uniform cell structure, lower density, and higher purity than competing technologies. The process has been refined over 100+ years (Croydon facility operating since 1923). New entrants face decades of process know-how to replicate at scale — the IP is partly patented but mostly tacit operational knowledge.
Designed-in to Nike shoe platforms (switching cost)
Nike’s footwear cushioning compounds (in Vaporfly, Pegasus, Alphafly franchises) are engineered around Zotefoams’ specific foam properties — density, energy return, durability. Switching supplier would require complete shoe re-design, re-testing, athlete validation. Multi-year cycle. Same dynamic in aerospace (ZOTEK F is FAR 25.853 fire-rated for cabin interiors — qualified into Boeing/Airbus programmes) and medical (ZOTEK N qualified for prosthetic / orthotic applications).
T-FIT cleanroom + ZOTEK F aerospace certifications (intangible)
T-FIT cleanroom insulation is qualified for semiconductor fab and pharmaceutical applications — multi-year approval cycles by end-customers (TSMC, Samsung, biopharma). ZOTEK F is one of very few foam materials passing FAA OSU (Ohio State University) heat-release test for aircraft interiors. Both certifications are barriers to entry that competitors must spend 2-4 years achieving.
Sustainability — "If the CEO is hit by a bus, does the moat survive?"
Genuine intangible-process moat — the autoclave manufacturing knowledge has been compounding inside the Croydon facility for 100+ years and survives any management change. Switching costs in footwear, aerospace, and cleanroom are real and structural. Financial signature confirms moat: ROIC trajectory 8% → 14.5% under Cox, op margin expanded from ~11% to 14.4% in two years, gross margins 28%+ in the foam business. The caveat that holds the moat back from a clean PASS: Nike alone is ~40% of revenue. A moat that depends on one customer’s continued commitment is structurally weaker than one that depends on thousands of customers — even if that one customer is locked in by spec. Dorsey would recognise this as a real moat with a concentrated revenue exposure, not a broken moat.

§ 8 — The Honest Conclusion

Why Not a Cevian Position — But Why It's Worth Knowing

Filter rejection — calibration reference, not a recommendation
Five structural rejections: (1) Cheque size — at £208m MC, even a 10% stake is £21m; below Cevian’s minimum position size by an order of magnitude. (2) Single-customer concentration — 40% of revenue from Nike means activist pressure on Nike-related decisions is impossible without risking the relationship; the operational levers Cevian usually pulls don’t apply. (3) Liquidity — modest daily volume; building a meaningful position would take months and exit similar. (4) Industry scope — specialty chemicals isn’t a core Cevian sector vs industrials/financials. (5) Strategy already in flight — the Cox-led "Expanding Beyond the Core" strategy is already executing; there’s no structural change for an activist to drive.

Where it does fit: a personal long-only portfolio at appropriate sizing. Quality compounder, 100-year moat, credible 2× revenue path, debt-light, founder-mentality leadership refreshed under Cox. Worth holding if you can stomach Nike-cycle volatility and AIM-style liquidity.
§ 8.1 — Joe's Take
Personal portfolio fit
This is the kind of name Andrew would respect — small-cap UK industrial with a real, ancient, hard-to-replicate moat (the Croydon autoclave process is genuinely 100 years old) trading at a reasonable multiple after a sentiment crash. The Nike concentration is the elephant; everything turns on whether you believe Nike-Zotefoams is a 10-year relationship or a 30-year one. The 2029 contract extension and Nike's investment alongside Zotefoams in Vietnam suggest the latter.

Sizing: 1-2% of a personal portfolio, accept that it'll trade with Nike's footwear cycle for the next 18 months. The 5-year compounding case (£300m / £60m → ~570p+ at unchanged multiple, more if rerated) is genuinely attractive. This belongs alongside Goodwin, IMI and Smiths in a UK-quality compounder allocation — not in a Cevian portfolio.
